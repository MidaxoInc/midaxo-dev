Takeaways:

  • Drift testing is being done on all TOFU content, and now represents 75% of all conversions
  • Average contact conversions pre-Drift was ~250/wk. Post drift ~180/wk (25% drop), but trending back to average.
  • Similar trend with net new logo conversions.
  • Qualitative feedback from sales is that the quality of conversions does not yet seem significantly higher
  • New company conversions took a dip in May due to Ad-words, but have rebounded in the past week
  • Total contact conversions have fallen dramatically, especially those sourced by email.

Questions / Recommendations:

  • Have we made any significant changes with Drift in the past 6 weeks that suggest it is "optimized?"
  • If not, then Drift may be continuing to weigh on total conversions, and we should scale back the Drift test to fewer TOFU forms until it can be fully optimized.
  • If we have, then we should proceed as is, and continue monitoring weekly conversion and new logo flow.
  • Regarding new conversions: the Drift roll-out and fall in net-new ad-words conversions happened at the same time. However, the # of web sessions sourced by ad-words did not fall of in May (report here), suggesting that the problem lies in conversions, not in sourced traffic.
  • Regarding contact conversions: Big takeaway is that something is going on with email conversions, dropping massively since Feb. Most of these have been conversions from existing companies, but still something to dig into.
